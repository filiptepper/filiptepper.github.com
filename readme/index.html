Hi, I’m Filip, and this is my README.

I am very excited to be working with you!

You & I

Every week we will have a 1:1. During this time I don’t expect project updates, this time is all about you. I will be giving you feedback, asking you for feedback, learning more about your goals, and most importantly - hearing how I can help you become the best you at $COMPANY. If you can’t do your job properly I need to know. If you have thoughts about your career I would love to know too. I’m here to help.

As we start working together I will ask you some very basic questions, like how you prefer to get your feedback, what made you pick the offer from $COMPANY, or how did you get into your career. I want to know you better.

I’m a big believer in continuous feedback. If I notice something you can expect to hear from me almost immediately. If you notice something - don’t wait until our 1:1, let me know that you want to talk. The small things are important too.

I never say “We need to talk” and then schedule a surprise meeting. All the meetings will have an agenda shared ahead.

I consider Slack to be asynchronous communication. I would encourage you to disable notifications and make sure to prioritize uninterrupted work. This goes both ways - I’m doing my best to be available but I need uninterrupted focus time too. I might call you, but only if it’s a business-threatening situation.

You are the expert. I will be asking questions that may seem obvious to you but will help me understand the context better. I work for you, and I need your guidance.

I’m a pretty serious person, so feel free to tell me to relax and remind me that we should be having fun too!

Me

I live in Warsaw, Poland.

I have 2 kids and I will often use them as an excuse for being AFK.

In my office, you’ll notice guitars & books. Besides that, you can hear me mention cycling and volleyball. And some nerdy stuff.

I am an introvert. Our first interaction will be awkward - I’m a really shy person.

I’ve been working across multiple time zones for the better part of the last 10 years and I will always prefer overcommunication over lack of visibility. But please let me know if you think it’s too much.

Looking forward to working with you!
